### Overall Evaluation

**1. Learning and working environment**

During my participation in the **First Cloud AI Journey** program, I feel this is a professional, serious, and clearly oriented learning environment. The content is organized by topics, making it easy for me to access knowledge from basic to advanced about AWS cloud computing. In addition, workshops and sharing sessions also helped me update a lot of practical knowledge, expanding my view of cloud technology and how AWS is applied in enterprises.

**2. Support from mentors and admin team**

Mentors and the admin team always supported me very enthusiastically throughout the internship. When encountering difficulties during learning or lab practice, I always received clear, easy-to-understand, and timely guidance. What I highly appreciate is that mentors not only gave direct answers but also guided me to think for myself and find ways to solve problems. This helped me develop proactivity and better problem-solving skills.

**3. Alignment with my major**

The internship content has a good connection with the Information Technology major I am studying. Through the program, I accessed important knowledge such as IAM, EC2, VPC, S3, RDS, Lambda, CloudWatch, and some system architecture models on AWS. This content helped me better understand how to build, deploy, and operate systems on the cloud platform, thereby supporting my career orientation in the future very well.

**4. Learning opportunities and skill development**

The program helped me improve both professional knowledge and soft skills. Professionally, I practiced many practical labs, better understood how to use AWS services and how to design systems in an optimized, secure, and scalable way. For soft skills, I developed self-learning, teamwork, problem presentation, report writing, and receiving feedback from mentors.

**5. Culture and team spirit**

I felt a very positive spirit of support and sharing in the program. Members were always ready to exchange and help each other when encountering difficulties during learning and practice. This environment made me feel more comfortable asking questions, sharing opinions, and learning from those around me.

**6. Policies and support for interns**

The program created good conditions for interns to learn and develop. Documents, workshops, labs, and guidance from mentors were all very useful. For me, the greatest value of the program lies not only in AWS knowledge but also in the opportunity to access practical work thinking, how to analyze problems, and how to build a career foundation in the Cloud/DevOps field.

### Some Other Questions

*   **What I'm  most satisfied with during the internship?**

    What I'm most satisfied with is being able to access and directly practice AWS services through specific labs. Being able to configure services myself, deploy resources, and understand how components in the system connect with each other helped me understand the lessons more deeply than just learning theory. In addition, the dedicated support from mentors also gave me more motivation to proactively learn and improve myself.

*   **What do I think the program could improve for future interns?**

    I think the program could add some group mini-projects at the end of each learning stage. This would give interns the opportunity to synthesize the knowledge they have learned, apply many AWS services to a more complete system, and develop more teamwork skills. In addition, if there are more sessions simulating real scenarios like system error handling, cost optimization, or cloud architecture design, the program will be even more practical.

*   **If recommending to friends, would I ddvise them to join this program? Why?**

    Yes. I would recommend this program to friends who have an orientation towards Cloud, DevOps, or System Architecture fields. The program not only provides foundational knowledge about AWS but also has many practical exercises helping learners understand how to apply it in practice. In addition, the professional learning environment, dedicated mentor support, and systematically built content are very suitable for students who want to develop in the technology field.

### Suggestions & Expectations

*   **Do I have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**

    I suggest the program could add more group practical exercises and have a final summary project at the end of the program. The final project could be a small mini-project, requiring the application of learned AWS services to build a basic system. This will help interns remember knowledge better and have a specific product to evaluate the learning process.

*   **Do I want to continue this program in the future?**

    If given the opportunity, I very much want to continue participating in more advanced content in the future. I am particularly interested in topics such as building system architectures on AWS, deploying CI/CD, DevOps, cloud security, system monitoring, and cost optimization. This is knowledge I think is very necessary for my career orientation in the future.
