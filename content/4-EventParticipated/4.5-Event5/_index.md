# "FCAJ Community Day - June 2026" Event Summary

## Event Objectives

**FCAJ Community Day - June 2026** is a community event organized to connect people interested in **AWS Cloud**, **Artificial Intelligence**, **DevOps**, **AI Agents** and modern technology trends. The event helps participants have a more practical view of how AI and Cloud are being applied in enterprises, especially in the areas of system operations, DevOps automation, building Voice Agents, optimizing work productivity and securing AI Agent connections.

Unlike a regular theoretical class, the program focuses on highly applicable topics, tied to real-world problems in modern cloud environments. Through sharing sessions and technical demos, participants better understand that Cloud is not just an infrastructure platform, but also an important environment for deploying intelligent AI systems that can analyze data, support decision-making and automate processes.

In addition to technical knowledge, the event also creates opportunities for participants to learn from experienced speakers, connect with the technology community and gain more direction for learning, personal projects as well as career development in the fields of Cloud, DevOps, Backend, Data and AI.

## Event Schedule

### 08:30 - 09:00: Check-in and Seating

Participants check-in, find their seats and prepare to attend the main presentation sessions of the event. This is also a time for everyone to interact, connect with the FCAJ community and meet members with the same direction in AWS Cloud, AI and modern technology.

### 09:00 - 09:25: Deep Response Engine: From Detection to Autonomous Resolution

**Speaker:** Nhat Tran

The first sharing session brings an interesting perspective on how systems using AI can go beyond traditional monitoring and alerting models. Instead of just detecting problems and sending notifications to the operations team, modern systems can go further by automatically detecting issues, analyzing causes and proposing appropriate handling directions.

This content emphasizes the **autonomous incident response** trend, where AI plays a supporting role for technical teams in system operations. In a cloud environment with many services, logs, metrics and alerts, manual processing often takes a lot of time. AI can help aggregate data, detect anomalies and shorten the incident analysis process.

Through this session, participants understand that cloud operations are not just about monitoring the system, but also need continuous observation, analysis, response and optimization capabilities. This is an important approach to help reduce downtime, reduce operating costs and improve system reliability.

### 09:25 - 09:55: Voice Agents: Building Human-Like AI Conversations at Scale

**Speakers:** Trung Vu, Kiet Tran and Nghi Danh

The sharing session about **Voice Agents** focuses on the future of conversational AI, especially systems that can communicate by voice more naturally. Modern Voice Agents are not just simple listening and answering tools, but also need to understand context, respond flexibly, maintain multi-turn conversations and have the ability to scale to serve many users at the same time.

A notable point is the difference between traditional IVR systems and AI Voice Agents. If IVR usually operates according to fixed scripts, Voice Agents can understand natural language and respond more flexibly according to user requirements. However, to deploy at scale, the system needs to solve many challenges such as latency, accuracy, voice quality, data security and the ability to integrate with backend business systems.

Through this session, participants see that Voice Agents are a very potential AI application direction, especially in customer care, smart hotlines, internal support and automating communication processes in enterprises.

### 09:55 - 10:20: AWS DevOps Agent: Your Always-Available Operations Teammate

**Speakers:** Bao Phan and Minh Nguyen Nguyen

This sharing session presents how AI can become an "always-available operations teammate" supporting the DevOps team. In reality, DevOps Engineers often have to handle many repetitive tasks such as checking logs, monitoring system status, monitoring resources, analyzing errors, checking pipelines and responding to incidents.

**AWS DevOps Agent** is introduced as an approach to help automate a part of operations tasks, thereby improving the technical team's work performance. Agents can support collecting information, analyzing operational data, providing processing suggestions and helping the troubleshooting process happen faster.

The important content of this part is using AI to reduce **MTTD** and **MTTR**, i.e., time to detect and resolve incidents. The main lesson is that AI does not replace DevOps Engineers, but supports people to work faster, more accurately and focus more on important decisions.

### 10:20 - 10:45: AI-Powered Productivity: Workforce Planning For Enterprise

**Speakers:** Truong Tran and Anh Dang

The **AI-Powered Productivity** sharing session expands the perspective from technical to business operations problems. The content focuses on how AI can support **workforce planning**, resource allocation and decision-making in organizations.

In enterprises, workforce planning is not just about managing the number of employees, but also related to team capacity, operational needs, work performance and long-term development strategy. AI can help aggregate data, analyze trends, detect bottlenecks and provide insights to help managers make better decisions.

This session helps participants understand that AI not only serves developers or infrastructure operations teams, but can also create value in management, human resources, finance and business operations activities. This is an important application direction in the digital transformation process.

### 10:45 - 11:30: Building Secure Private MCP Connection with Amazon Quick

**Speakers:** Toan Nguyen and Nghi Danh

The last session goes deep into the topic of building private and secure connections through **MCP - Model Context Protocol** with Amazon Quick. The content emphasizes the importance of **security-first** architecture when deploying modern AI systems in cloud environments.

When AI Agents have the ability to connect to data, tools and internal systems, security becomes a mandatory factor. If access rights are not well controlled, agents can access sensitive data or perform actions beyond the allowed scope. Therefore, designing private connections, limiting permissions, logging and monitoring agent activities is very important.

Through this sharing session, participants understand that a good AI system not only needs to be smart and efficient, but also must be safe, controlled, have monitoring capabilities and clear traceability. This is an important foundation when bringing AI into real enterprise environments.

## Key Highlights

The first highlight of the event is how AI is applied to cloud operations and DevOps. Topics like **Deep Response Engine** and **AWS DevOps Agent** show that AI can support detecting incidents, analyzing causes, proposing processing directions and reducing manual operations in the system operation process.

The second highlight is the development of **AI Agents** from simple chatbots to systems that can communicate, call tools and handle real tasks. Especially, Voice Agents show that AI can create more natural conversation experiences, better supporting customer care, smart hotlines and communication processes in enterprises.

The third highlight is the important role of security when deploying AI. When AI Agents have the ability to access data or perform actions, principles like **least privilege**, **private connectivity**, **logging**, **monitoring** and **audit trail** need to be applied seriously to ensure system safety.

## What You Learned

Through the event, participants better understand that modern AI is not just a content creation tool, but is becoming an important part of system architecture, operational processes and business activities. An effective AI solution needs a stable cloud infrastructure, good security, scalability, monitoring capabilities and cost control.

The event also helps participants reinforce cloud architecture thinking. When building AI systems on AWS, you not only need to care about the AI model, but also have to design the right foundation components like compute, networking, IAM, monitoring, logging, storage and database.

In addition, participants are more aware of the role of DevOps Automation. AI can support technical teams in handling repetitive tasks, analyzing operational data and responding to incidents faster. However, the more access rights AI has, the tighter control is needed to ensure safety.
